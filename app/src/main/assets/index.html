FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear, direct answers to common pediatric dental questions parents ask us.

When should my child first visit a pediatric dentist?

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends scheduling your child's first visit by their first birthday, or within 6 months of their very first baby tooth coming through. Early visits help us monitor development and catch dental issues before they grow.

Is dental sedation or laughing gas safe for toddlers?

Yes, absolutely. Nitrous oxide (laughing gas) is highly researched, extremely safe, and mild. It is gently inhaled through a pediatric mask, helping children stay relaxed during treatments. It wears off completely within 5 minutes of breathing clean oxygen.

What makes a pediatric dentist different from a family dentist?

Pediatric dentists complete 2 to 3 years of extra, specialized hospital-grade training after finishing dental school. This focuses specifically on child psychology, toddler behavior management, children's physical development, and dental care for children with special medical needs.

Do you accept dental insurance, and what are the payment options?

We accept most major dental PPO insurance plans. We file all paperwork directly on your behalf to maximize your benefits. For out-of-pocket costs, we accept cash, credit cards, and offer interest-free monthly payment plans.
